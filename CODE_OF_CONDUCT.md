# Code Of Conduct

InterMed is an experimental compatibility project, which means contributors
will often arrive with broken launches, unclear logs, and strong opinions about
loaders. We want that energy to become useful evidence, not noise.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and patient with alpha testers, mod authors, and maintainers.
- Assume good intent, especially when someone reports an incomplete failure.
- Critique behavior, code, claims, or evidence; do not attack people.
- Keep discussions focused on reproducible facts, logs, diagnostics, and next
  steps.
- Respect privacy. Do not pressure users to publish private logs, tokens,
  server addresses, or modpack contents.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, slurs, threats, or sexualized language.
- Dismissing reports because a user is new, uses a different launcher, or cannot
  immediately produce perfect diagnostics.
- Publishing private vulnerability details before maintainers have had a fair
  chance to triage them.
- Deliberately derailing issues or PRs with unrelated loader, platform, or
  community arguments.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or delete comments; close issues; request
changes in tone; or block users when needed to keep the project safe and useful.
For security-sensitive or conduct-sensitive reports, prefer private maintainer
contact when possible.

This code of conduct is intentionally short. The spirit is simple: be precise,
be kind, and help turn alpha chaos into actionable signal.
